"Cervical cancer: An NCD we can overcome" • 2018
|19 May| 3.30 pm – 6.30 pm Geneva Starling Hotel

Cervical cancer (CC) programs should benefit from innovative approaches to access additional financial and technical support. This third round table will show the way to accelerate action and value the successful factors, given the resources already available and new partners likely to be engaged. This year, we hope to come up with a road map for measuring outcomes and identifying bottlenecks over the year to come. Based on selected case studies, we will provide a source of guidance through an exchange of best practices within our network.

"Cervical cancer : An NCD we can overcome" • 2017
21 May; 2.30 pm – 5.30 pm Geneva Starling Hotel

This upcoming roundtable discussion will bring the issue of cervical cancer to the forefront of political leaders, allow the exchange of experiences, and highlight the leaders’ key roles in moving forward.
As such, the high level ministerial panel discussion will help keep up the current momentum to eliminate cervical cancer as a public health concern by 2030, with it being one of the more achievable Sustainable Development Goals.
The discussion will also feature innovative approaches and partnerships as well as new methods and tools to build capacity and advance cervical cancer control.

"Cervical cancer: An NCD we can overcome" • 2016
11 May; 2.30 pm – 5.30 pm Geneva Starling Hotel

Prior to the sixty-ninth World Health Assembly, held in Geneva, from 23 to 28 May 2016, the roundtable enabled Commonwealth member countries’ delegates to participate in the discussion. An interactive exchange provided an overview of current interventions and research as it relates to cervical cancer in Africa.
The report of our roundtable 2016 includes the presentations of the speakers, and the main points of discussion. It is a practical tool to provide guidance for those involved in related issues. Keeping with this mindset, it is also designed to match with the ongoing discussions on our social media networks to consistently include new ideas and valuable suggestions and comments.

"Cervical cancer: an NCD we can overcome":

More on GHO's 2017 Roundtable

We have the knowledge, tools, and methods to achieve the objectives:

• HPV vaccination is the most important advance in cervical cancer prevention
• Leveraging current programs/resources is cost effective (HIV-HPV, HPV- RH)
• Partnerships are needed and possible to reinforce the continuum of care
• The Sustainable Development Goals aimed at reducing premature mortality from NCDs by one third by 2030, helps create a new momentum towards accelerating action.

Overview of GHO's 2017 Roundtable


• SPEAKERS: Political leaders, and various experienced professionals from the public and private sector, as well as the civil society and development partners.
• AUDIENCE: About 100 participants: Political and global health leaders, financial and health experts, development partners, researchers, NGOs, and media.
• EXPECTED OUTCOMES: The sharing of experiences will create a climate of informed decision‐making and will contribute to keep up the current new momentum towards increasing women’s access to cervical cancer screening, prevention and treatment.
• FORMAT: A panel discussion is the most appropriate format to enable speakers to best exchange their experiences with the audience and further discuss opportunities to join forces. Experienced experts will facilitate the panel discussion.
• ORGANIZERS: lobal Health Objectives is a Swiss nonprofit association dedicated to non communicable diseases control, especially cervical cancer, The Université Numerique Francophone Mondale, a French nonprofit organization, is committed to creating bridges between Anglophone and Francophone countries to extensively build capacity in all fields related to cancer control and women’s health, through distance training and new communication technologies.
